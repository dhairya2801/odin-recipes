Biryani

This is a traditional Indian rice-based dish enjoyed all across the country, though it has its roots in Mughal cuisine. Biryani is highly aromatic, rich in flavors, and often prepared for special occasions or weekend dinners. It is usually paired with Lassi as the side. Lassi is a refreshing yogurt-based drink, perfectly balancing the spices of the Biryani with its cool sweetness and creamy thickness. I can confirm that after this royal combination, you are sure to feel completely satisfied and at peace. Check the recipe below to make it close to perfection!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes) or paneer
  • 2 Onions
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • Green chillies
  • Ginger garlic paste
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, cardamon)
  • Cumin seeds
  • Masala
    • Red chilli powder
    • Turmeric powder
    • Garam masala
    • Salt
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Oil or ghee

Steps

  1. Wash and soak basmatic rice for 30 minutes. Parboil it with salt and drain.
  2. Heat ghee/oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and whole spices.
  3. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger garlic paste, green chillies, and tomatoes, cook till soft.
  5. Mix in all masalas along with chopped vegetables/paneer. Cook for 5–7 minutes.
  6. In a heavy-bottom pan, layer rice and cooked masala alternately. Top with coriander and mint leaves.
  7. Cover tightly and cook on low flame (“dum”) for 15 minutes.
